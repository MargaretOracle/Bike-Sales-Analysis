# Bike-Sales-Analysis

## Project Overview
This project is a deep dive into a high-end bike shop that was doing great until it hit a major speed jump. In 2016 and 2017, the business was booming,but by 2018, sales suddenly dropped by nearly **half (47%)**

I used **Bike Shop Dataset** to play a detective. I wanted to find out if the shop was losing customers, running out of popular bikes, or if the staff just couldn't keep up with the work.
By lookig at three years of sales, inventor and shipping dta, i identified exactly where the money was leaking and how to plug the holes for 2019.

I broke my findings into three clear sections:
     1. **The Executive Dashboard** - The page acts as a quick overview or summary of the business entire ecosystem.
     2. **Inside Inventory** - This page focuses on product & store breakdown.
     3. **Staff Performance** - This page shifts the focus from "What" is being sold to "Who" is selling it.(Analyzes how staff members are handling the workload and how their indiviadual performance affects total sales volume).
     4. **Staff Drill Through** This is a detailed page that allows the user to click on any staff member to see their full profile.

## The Excutive dashboard
### Core Business Questions Addressed
1. **Total Revenue** : What is the total revenue generated per year, and how does each year contribute to the overall revenue across the three-year period? 
2. **Growth Sustainability** : Is our **$7.69M** lifetime revenue a sign of healthy scaling?
3. **The 2018 Crash** : What triggered the **47% Year-over-Year** drop from **$3.45M(2017) to 1.81M(2018)?
4. **Product Vatility** : Which products/categories/brands performs well in terms of revenue generation?
5. **Monthly Sales** : Are there specific months where bike sales spikes?
6. **Operational Spikes** : Are our 10 staffs and 3 store locations capable of handling high volume seasonal spikes?

### Key Observations(The "What")
1. #### The Revenue Trajectory
   - **The 2016 Baseline** : Estabished a stable foundation of **$2.43M**
   - **The 2017 Peak** : Scaled rapidly to **$3.45M** (*42% growth*)
   - **The 2018 decline** : Collapsed to **$1.81M** (*47% decline)
   - **The $5.87M Benchmark** : This "Last Year" figure represents our total momentum(*Sum of 2016 and 2017 revenue*) before the 2018 crash, proving 2018 is an extreme outliers
     
2. #### Market and Brand Compositon
   - **Brand Monopoly** : A single brand **Trek**, accounts for nearly **$5M** of all time revenue
   - **Category shift** : All-time , **Mountain Bikes ($2.71M)** lead the shop. However, in 2018, **Road Bikes ($619K)** overtook Mountain Bikes for the first time.
   - **Top Performing Products** : The **Trek Slash 8 ($555K) is our highest-grossing product, but it is a 2016 model.
